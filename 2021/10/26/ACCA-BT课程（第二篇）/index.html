ACCA - BT（Business and Technology）
Chapter2：Business Environment
In this chapter , we will study many models which are used to analyse different business environments .

Introduce

Macro environment - PEST/PESTEL Model
Micro environment - Poter’s Five Forces Model
Orgnisation - Value chain
Micro with Orgnisation - SWOT Model

PEST/PESTEL Model

PEST Model

P : Political and legal
E : Economic
S : Social and demographic
T : Technological

PESTEL Model

P : Political
E : Economic
S : Social and demographic
T : Technological
E : Environmental
L : Legal

Description

Political :
Government to Orgnisation : Government can directly affect organisation through various measures such as political stability , capacity expansion , market competition , entry barries , etc .
Orgnisation to Government : a) Employ lobbyists to influence key individual ministers . b) Try to influence public opinion , and hence the legislative agenda .
Economic :
As for macro-economic environment , organisations cannot run in a vacuum - they make decisions within a large and complex economic environment .
Social :
a) Demographic trend b) Family Life Cycle c) Social structures and class d) Buying partterns e) Cultural trend .
Technological :
a) General effects b) Orgnisation structure c) Outsourcing
To understand b option , we will introduce a concept called span of control , it refers to the number of subordinates directly responsible to a superior . As a leader , we can work with high-technology to control the subordinates , then achieve the organisation-delayering .
To understand c option , here is the definition : the contracting out of specified operations or services to an external vendor . And here are the different types :
1.Ad-hoc : for a short term , 2.Project : a particular IT project , 3.Partial : some IT service , 4.Total : the vast majoring of IT sevice .
Outsourcing’s Advantages and Disadvantages:
AD : · Removing uncertainty cost · Economies of scale · Flexibility · New skills and technology
DISAD : · Too important · Locked in contract · Confidential information · Missing competitive advantages · Ineffective management
Environmental :
Economic activities to Environment : 1.deoletion of natural resoucrces 2.noise 3.residual air and waster esstion
Environment to Economic activities : 1.waste management 2.compliance costs or remediation costs 3.environment training 4.legal costs and fines 5.record keeping and reporting
CSR (Corporate social responsibility)
Legal :
1.Employment protection law 2.Data protection and security law 3.Health and safety law 4.Consumer protection law
Then here are some things about the termination of employment :
a) retirement b) resignation c) dismissal d) redundancy
In the below cases , we accept the retirement :
promotion opportunity for younger employees ; early retirement is an alternative to redundancy ; balance the age structure ; reduce the pension costs
For the resignation , there must have the period of notice to leave should be set out in the contract of employment .
And about dismissal , we differ it into 3 types :
constructive dismissal ; wrongful dismissal ; unfair dismissal .
For the redundancy , we got two points :
the employer has ceased to carry on the business ; the requirement of the business for employees .
It is important to notic that , employees is not entitled to compensation in these cases :
the employee unresonably rejected the offer ; the employee is of pensionable age or over , or has less than two years continuous employment .
Then here are some things about the data protection :
Data protection and security law aims to protect individual privacy and harmonise data protection legislation . Here we will study the UK Data Protection Act 8 principle :
1.processed fairly and lawfully 2.processed in accordace with the rights of data subjects 3.obtained only for one or more specified and lawful purposes 4.adequate . relevant and not exceeding the purpose why they are processed 5.accurate and kept up to date 6.not be kept longer than necessary 7.measures against unauthorised processing 8.Not be transferred outside EU .
And it admit these rights :
1.seeking compensation or suing for damage and any associated distress 2.applying to the courts to correct inaccurate data or even wiping off the data 3.obtain access to personal data of which he or she is the subject 4.sue a data user for any damage or distress caused to him by personal data about him which is incorrect or misleading .
Then here are some things about health and security law :
a) the employer has legal obligations b) accident and illness cost employers’ money c) the companys’ image may suffer
Then here are some things about contract law :
a contract is a legal binding agreement which means if one party fails to carry out the agreement , the other party can take legal action against him for breach of contract .

Poter’s Five Forces Model

The five forces of Poter’s Five Forces Model are rivalry among existing firms , threat of entrants , threat of subsitute products or service , bargaining power of customers , bargaining power of suppliers .
a)rivalry among existing firms :
The competitive rivalry describes the level of intensity among the existing compentitors in this industry , which as a result impacts the profit they could generate .
b)threat of new entrants :
1.barries to entry : switching cost , economy of scale , product differentiation , capital requirement .
2.existing competitors’ responses .
c)threat of subsitute product or service :
1.the quality of subsitute is better and switching cost is negligible 2.the loyalty of customer is low
d)bargaiing power of suppliers :
1.suppliers rely on us 2.number of suppliers 3.switching cost 4.differentiated product
e)bargaining power of buyers :
1.purchase quantity 2.number of buyers 3.switching cost 4.standard items or specialised 5.the price-awareness of consumers

Value Chain

Poter’s value chain is a collection of activities that are performed by a company to create value for its customers .
it has support activities and primary activities .
Primary activities :
Inbound Logistics , Operations , Outbound Logistics , Marketing and sales , service .
Support activities :
Firm Infrastructure , Human resource management , Technology development , Procurement .

SWOT Analysis Model

S : strength
W : weakness
O : opportunity
T : threat
SWOT Analysis examines the strength , weakness , opportunity and threat of an organisation .
Unlike PEST forcuses on external environmental issues , SWOT analysis , which focuses on the internal and external situation .
