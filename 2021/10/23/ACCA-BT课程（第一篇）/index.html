ACCA - BT（Business and Technology）
Chapter1：Organisation and their stakeholders
As the first lesson of the ACCA series , this blog （or the lesson BT） aim to introduce the basic things to us . And now , we will begin our journey with the concept of organisation .

Organisation：

Definition

Definition：An organisation is a social arrangement for achieving controlled performance towards collective goals that creates value and which has a boundary separating it from its environment .
In the definition of organisation , there are four key words :
· collective goals
· social arrangement
· controlled performance
· boundary
What organisation brings to us ? in the other word , what’s the benefits of organisation ? Well , such as :
Time saving 、 Breakthrough of individual limitations 、 Specialisation 、 Knowledge accumulation and sharing , etc .
In conclusion , the benefits can be summarised as one word , synergy , which indicates the total output of people working together will exceed the total input of people working separately . Simply speaking , it means 1 + 1 > 2 .
Now we can see , organisation has these characteristics :
· systems and procedures enable control
· Different people specialise in different activity
· A variety of objects and goals
· Inputs - process - Outputs
· Synergy

Classification

Here is the third point to study organisation : the classification of organisation . In fact , we have many ways to differ organisations :
· Size
· Control
· Activity
· Source of finance
· Technology
But today , we are major in these ways :
· Profit or non-profit orientation
· Ownership
· Legal status
Classifying organisations by profit orientation:
1.Profit seeking organisations(commercial)
Profit seeking organisations’ primary goal is maximize profit , their secondary goal is providing goods and services .
2.Not-for-profit organisations
NFPs’ primary goal is providing goods and services , and their secondary goal is minimize cost . At the same time , due to most of NFPs depend on social donation , they have to make sure the money value for money .
Classifying organisations by ownership
1.Public sector
Organisations owned or run by central or local government or government agencies .
Public sector’s advantagements and disadvantagements:
AD: · fairness/filling the gap · economies of scale · cheaper finance
DISAD: · accountabitity · cost · interference
2.Private sector
Organisations not owned or run by central or local government or government agencies .
And here are the classifications of the Private sector：
A）Non-governmental organisations
A NGO is a legally constituted organisation of people acting together independently from any government .
B）Co-operatives && Mutual Associations
Co-operatives are businesses owned by their workers or customers ; Mutual Associations are owned by their members such as the credit union formed by financial institutions .
Classifying organisations by legal status
1.Sole trader
A sole trader , also known as a sole proprietorship , is the simplest business arrangement , in which one individual runs and owns the entire business .
2.Partnership
A partnership is the relationship existing between two or more persons who join to carry on a trade or business . Each person contributes money , property , labour or skill , and expects to share in the profits and losses of the business .
3.Limited liability companies
In a limited liability company , shareholders’ liability is limited to the capital they originally invested . If such a company becomes insolvent , the shareholders’ personal assets remain protected .
Limited liability companies’ advantagements and disadvantagements:
AD: · seperate legal personality · reduces risk · more money · ownership and control
DISAD: · legal compliance costs · shareholders have little practical power
About LC , here are some types of it :
A）Private limited company （Ltd）
B）Public limited company （Plc）
The difference is whether they are listed or not .
Besides , here are some differences of them :
a. number of shareholders
b. transferability of shares
c. control and ownership
d. access of finance

Stakeholder：

In order to make stakeholders’ types clear , we will introduce a model called ICE model .

ICE model

I:internal
stakeholders in company : such as employees 、 managers or directors .
C:connected
stakeholders as inputs and outputs : stakeholders as inputs like shareholders , creditors and suppliers . stakeholders as outputs like customers .
E:external
stakeholders like community-at-large , pressur-groups , government and trade union .
Among the stakeholders of a company , there are many conflicts :
Customers versus shareholders : Product , price/quality/sevice , levels , versus profits/dividends .
Gerenal public versus shareholders : Effect on the environment versus profit/dividends .
Shareholders versus managers : shareholders prefer long-term return while managers usually seek short-term profits and bonus .

Mendelow matrix

To deal with different stakeholders , we’ll introduce Mendelow matrix :
In this model , we use power and interest to judge stakeholders .
Low Power && Low Interest : Minimal effort
Low Power && High Interest : Keep informed
High Power && Low Interest : Keep satisfied
High Power && High Interest : Key player
This model common manifested as a matrix .
